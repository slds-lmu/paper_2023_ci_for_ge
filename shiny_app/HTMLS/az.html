Besides the main benchmark study, we conducted a separate experiment that compared the variance estimator from the ROCV inference method (Austern and Zhou) with the true variance of the CV point estimate. We used a linear regression model on datasets simulated from a distribution following the relationship below: $$Y_i = X_i + E_i, \quad \text{where } i = 1, \ldots, n, \quad X_i \overset{i.i.d.}{\sim} \mathcal{N}(0, 1), \quad E_i \overset{i.i.d.}{\sim} \mathcal{N}(0, 0.04)$$ The experiment was repeated for different choices of $$n \in \{500, 1000, 5000, 10000\}$$. To estimate the true variance of the (5-fold) cross-validation, we obtained 100 cross-validation point estimates on independently simulated datasets. The variance estimator for the ROCV method was repeated 10 times.
